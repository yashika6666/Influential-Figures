Social Reformers of India

Raja Ram Mohan Roy

Known as the 'Father of the Indian Renaissance,' Raja Ram Mohan Roy was a key figure in the abolition of the Sati practice. His advocacy for women's rights and social reforms marked the beginning of progressive change in India.

Jyotirao Phule

A prominent social reformer from Maharashtra, Jyotirao Phule worked tirelessly for the education and upliftment of lower castes and women. He co-founded India's first school for girls with his wife, Savitribai Phule.

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Known as the architect of the Indian Constitution, Dr. Ambedkar was a crusader against untouchability and caste discrimination. His efforts for social justice and equality continue to inspire marginalized communities in India.

Savitribai Phule

As one of India’s first female teachers, Savitribai Phule was a pioneer in women’s education and fought against gender and caste-based discrimination. Her efforts laid the foundation for future women's rights movements in India.

Swami Vivekananda

A social reformer and spiritual leader, Swami Vivekananda sought to address social ills like poverty and inequality through education and spiritual upliftment. His teachings continue to influence modern-day India.
